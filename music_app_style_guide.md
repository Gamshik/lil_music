# STYLE GUIDE --- Music App

### Modern (Apple) × Retro Emotion

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Core Idea

This product lives between **precision and memory**.\
Between **a flawless digital object** and **a cultural artifact of music
history**.

The foundation is calm, confident, modern --- almost invisible in its
perfection.\
On top of that, subtle traces of retro appear --- not as decoration, but
as *emotion leaking through the system*.

This is not nostalgia.\
This is **controlled nostalgia** --- curated, intentional, and
restrained.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Visual Philosophy

Think of the interface as:

-   a polished black object in a dim room\
-   light sliding across curved glass\
-   sound translated into surface\
-   silence that makes music louder

The UI should feel:

-   quiet, but not empty\
-   minimal, but not cold\
-   premium, but not sterile\
-   expressive, but never loud

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Balance

Modern should dominate.\
Retro should **interrupt gently**.

Modern is the structure.\
Retro is the aftertaste.

If you remove retro --- the product still works.\
If you remove modern --- the product collapses.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Color Direction

Color is not paint.\
Color is **light behavior**.

### Base Palette (Modern Core)

-   Deep Black --- #0B0B0D\
-   Graphite --- #1C1C1F\
-   Soft Charcoal --- #2A2A2E\
-   Silver Gray --- #B8B8BD\
-   Soft White --- #F5F5F7

These colors should feel:

-   dense\
-   soft\
-   expensive\
-   slightly warm or neutral (never harsh)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Accent Palette (Controlled Energy)

Use sparingly. Always intentional.

-   Electric Blue --- #4DA3FF\
-   Cyan Glow --- #5CF2FF\
-   Soft Violet --- #8A7CFF\
-   Warm Chrome Yellow --- #FFC84D\
-   Subtle Red-Orange --- #FF5A36

Accents should behave like:

-   reflections\
-   signals\
-   energy pulses\
-   not backgrounds

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Spectral / Prism Effects

Inspired by light through glass:

-   gradients that shift subtly\
-   thin color fringes\
-   chromatic edges\
-   iridescent hints

These should feel:

-   physical\
-   optical\
-   almost accidental

Never decorative.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Retro Editorial Tones (Rare Use)

Used only in emotional or curated contexts:

-   Cream --- #F2EFEA\
-   Dusty Beige --- #D8D2C8\
-   Vintage Yellow --- #E2B84B\
-   Deep Ink --- #111111\
-   Washed Red --- #C94A3F

These colors bring:

-   warmth\
-   memory\
-   print-like feeling

But should never dominate system UI.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Typography Direction

Typography should feel like:

-   a voice that knows when to speak and when to stay silent

### Modern Layer

-   neutral\
-   precise\
-   calm\
-   invisible in the best way

It carries structure and clarity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Retro Layer

-   expressive\
-   editorial\
-   slightly nostalgic\
-   cultural

It appears rarely, but when it does --- it changes the tone.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Atmosphere

The interface should feel like:

-   night listening\
-   focused attention\
-   private space\
-   curated experience

Not a dashboard.\
Not a tool.\
A **place**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Texture & Material

Everything should feel like it exists in space:

-   soft reflections\
-   subtle depth\
-   restrained highlights\
-   almost-touchable surfaces

Avoid obvious textures.\
Favor **perception over decoration**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Motion

Motion is where emotion lives.

It should be:

-   slow enough to feel\
-   fast enough to not interrupt\
-   smooth like sound

Transitions should feel like:

-   sliding glass\
-   shifting air\
-   fading light

Never mechanical.\
Never abrupt.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Retro Presence

Retro should feel like:

-   a memory appearing briefly\
-   a visual echo\
-   a cultural whisper

Not:

-   a theme\
-   a costume\
-   a layer applied everywhere

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Creative Mindset

Design as if:

You are building a device that doesn't exist yet.\
But somehow feels familiar.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Final Feeling

When everything is done right, the user should feel:

"I don't notice the interface.\
But I feel it."

And more importantly:

"The music feels better here."
